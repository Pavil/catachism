The Codex Amiatinus

The Codex Amiatinus is the sole surviving artifact of three containing both Old and New Testaments, produced in the early 8th century AD by Anglo-Saxon Benedictine monks from the monastaries of Wearmouth and Jarrow in Northumbria of Northern England. This codex is roughly a foot thick and weighs 75 lbs. This time period introduces several innovations, such as spacing between words, and starting sentences on a new line.
